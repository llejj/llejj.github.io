HMM Stocks

I used a Hidden Markov Model to analyze historical $SPY data. My model had two hidden states, and its emissions were the daily % price changes. Results below.

It seems that State 0 reflects more stable bull markets, while State 1 reflects increased volatility during bear markets. This model offers a simple description of the stock market. So yeah, HMM's are kinda cool.

HMM Speech Recognition

I implemented a Hierarchical Hidden Markov Model (HHMM) based on "The Hierarchical Hidden Markov Model: Analysis and Applications" by SHAI FINE, YORAM SINGER, and NAFTALI TISHBY. Specifically, I implemented the Baum-Welch and Viterbi algorithms.

Then, I tried to train an HHMM for speech recognition. Unfortunately, the final training took way too long. The Generalized Baum-Welch runs in O(__). In the future, I plan on implementing the Greedy ... algorithm as described in [other paper], as well as optimizing the tree-structure of the HHMM.
